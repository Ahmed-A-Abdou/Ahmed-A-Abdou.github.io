EGY Game Jam 🏆 1st Place — Advanced Mechanics Team Project 24 Hours

Artillery Aftermath

The Goal
Built in under 24 hours for the EGY Game Jam, Artillery Aftermath is a game centered on a single satisfying mechanic — an artillery cannon that fires through a low-poly canyon environment and physically destroys everything in its path. The challenge was delivering a complete, polished gameplay experience within the jam time limit while making the core mechanic feel genuinely impactful.
What I Built
  • Full scene setup — camera placement, lighting, and environment composition in the low-poly canyon
  • Artillery mechanic with aiming and firing controls
  • Real-time trajectory ray showing the projectile's predicted path before firing
  • Destructible environment system — structures physically break apart and scatter on impact
  • Projectile physics with accurate collision and impact response
What I Learned
A 24-hour jam is a completely different kind of pressure than a normal project. Every decision has to be fast and deliberate — there's no time to over-engineer. I learned how to scope tightly around one mechanic and make it feel great rather than building broadly and shipping something half-finished. The destructible environment system was the hardest part to get right under time pressure, and getting it to feel satisfying by the deadline was the most rewarding part of the experience. Working with a 3D artist also showed me how much cleaner a project runs when responsibilities are clearly split from the start.
