Unity Pathway Unit 2 Top-Down

Feed the Animals

About this project
Unit 2 prototype from the Junior Programmer Pathway — a top-down survival game where animals charge toward the player from multiple directions. The player throws food to satisfy them before they reach the screen edge. Extended beyond the base lesson with a hunger bar system that adds a second layer to the survival mechanic.
What I Built
  • Top-down player movement with directional aiming and rotation
  • Food projectile thrown toward incoming animals
  • Animals spawning from the top and both sides of the map
  • Hunger bar on each animal using a UI Slider — animals despawn when fully fed
  • Collision detection between food and animals
  • Game over when an animal reaches the screen edge
What I Learned
Extending the base lesson pushed me to think about spawn management across multiple screen edges — not just one direction. The hunger bar introduced me to UI Sliders as a gameplay mechanic rather than just a settings tool, and connecting it to despawn logic taught me how UI state and game logic need to communicate cleanly. Managing multiple simultaneous animals made me more deliberate about how I structure per-object behaviour.
