Unity Pathway Unit 2 Top-Down

Feed the Animals

About this project
Unit 2 prototype from the Junior Programmer Pathway. A top-down survival game where animals randomly spawn and charge toward the player, who must throw food to stop them before they reach you.
What I Built
  • Top-down player movement with rotation toward mouse/direction
  • Projectile throwing mechanic to launch food at incoming animals
  • Animal spawning system with randomized positions and charge behavior
  • Collision detection between food projectiles and animals
  • Game over trigger when an animal reaches the player
What I Learned
This project introduced me to top-down game logic, which requires a different approach to movement and aiming than third-person or side-scrolling games. I got comfortable with projectile instantiation, applying forces in a direction, and handling collisions between moving objects. It also pushed me to think about spawn management — how to control difficulty through spawn rate and position randomization.
